At-risk students are close to failing and need support



3\. Absences Affect Risk Level

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_



Insight 3 – Higher Absences Increase Risk



From Absences vs At-Risk Students scatter plot:



At-risk students generally show higher absence values



Students with fewer absences are mostly not at risk.



Conclusion:

Attendance is a key factor in identifying at-risk students.



4\. Study Time and Risk

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_



Insight 4 – Low Study Time Leads to Higher Risk



From Study Time vs At-Risk chart:



Students with lower studytime levels are more likely to be at risk.



Conclusion:

Increasing study time may reduce student risk.



5\. Past Failures and Risk

\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_



Insight 5 – Students with Failures Are More At Risk



From Failures vs At-Risk chart:



Students with past failures are more likely to be at risk



Students with zero failures are less likely at risk



Conclusion:

Past failures strongly predict academic risk.
